Introduction to GNU Radio

The GNU Radio project is creating a free software defined radio (SDR). We're designing the software (and maybe a little hardware) to enable experimentation with the latest digital wireless protocols.

What is a Software Defined Radio?

Joe Mitoloa says, "A software radio is a radio whose channel modulation waveforms are defined in software. That is, waveforms are generated as sampled digital signals, converted from digital to analog via a wideband DAC and then possibly upconverted from IF to RF. The receiver, similarly, employs a wideband Analog to Digital Converter (ADC) that captures all of the channels of the software radio node. The receiver then extracts, downconverts and demodulates the channel waveform using software on a general purpose processor." [1]

For our purposes, on the receive side, the idea is to get a wide band ADC as close to the antenna as is convenient, get the samples into something we can program, and then grind on them in software.

What could I do with a Software Defined Radio?

  • You could learn more about DSP and Digital Communications than just about everybody.
  • You could listen to all kinds of interesting things that most general purpose radios can't demodulate.
  • You could experiment with new modulation strategies in the unlicensed bands.
  • You could build an encrypting radio transceiver.
  • You could explore the security aspects of wireless LANS.

What's the status of GNU Radio?

GNU Radio is currently under development. We are in the design and prototyping stage, and have not yet made an official release.

How can I contribute?

The GNU Radio project is hosted on Savannah, an FSF SourceForge clone. The project home page can be found at http://savannah.gnu.org/projects/gnuradio, where links to other project related information can be found, including directions for CVS access to the development tree.

Mailing lists and contacts

To report a bug in GNU Radio, please send mail to bug-gnuradio@gnu.org.

For help with GNU Radio, send mail to help-gnuradio@gnu.org.

There is also a developer oriented mailing list devoted to discussion of GNU Radio. To subscribe, send a message to discuss-gnuradio-request@gnu.org.
